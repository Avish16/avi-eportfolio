Program overview

How the M.S. in Applied Data Science shaped my technical work, research approach, and communication skills.

What “learning outcomes” mean here

Graduate data science programs typically aim for a few shared goals: you should be able to work responsibly with real data, apply rigorous methods, build systems that others can trust and reuse, and explain your work clearly to people who are not specialists. The bullets below are phrased in plain language so reviewers (including non-experts) can see what I took from Syracuse’s M.S. Applied Data Science and how I know I have grown.

Program learning outcomes (student-friendly)

  1. Foundations: Use statistics, machine learning, and deep learning where needed to extract signal from messy, real-world data.
  2. Systems thinking: Design pipelines that move from raw inputs through cleaning, modeling, evaluation, and visualization or deployment.
  3. Communication: Present methods and limitations honestly, with visuals and language suited to business or public audiences.
  4. Responsible AI: Consider privacy, safety, validation, and failure modes, especially when models affect people’s decisions.
  5. Domain application: Connect methods to a problem context (healthcare, geospatial policy, markets, operations) rather than stopping at accuracy on a leaderboard.

High-level summary of portfolio projects

  • MediExplain: A human-centered medical AI system that uses retrieval-augmented generation, modular LLM agents, and output validation for reliable explanations.
  • Driver drowsiness detection: A computer vision pipeline trained on infrared eye images to classify awake versus sleepy states and detect sustained drowsiness.
  • DataThon (Syracuse spatial analytics): A team project that links city code violations and assessment data to produce grid-level risk and vacancy maps.
  • Bitcoin flash crash prediction: A minute-level modeling workflow combining market features and Reddit sentiment to identify flash crash events.

Additional coursework and professional work, including my MoMacMo internship on large-scale sensing data and my Healthcare AI research, reinforce the same habits at production and research scale.
